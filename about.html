About Me

Building at the
intersection
of AI & robotics

Engineering student, builder, and curious scientist.

I'm Kanishk Patel, an engineering student with a deep interest in machine learning, robotic simulation, and computational mechanics. My work spans from training classification models to designing and simulating robotic arms in ROS 2 and MATLAB.

I enjoy projects where software meets physics — whether it's trajectory control of a 2-DOF arm or kinematic analysis of a quick-return mechanism. I believe the best engineers are also curious scientists.

Currently building my portfolio of ML and robotics projects, with a growing focus on deep learning, computer vision, and intelligent autonomous systems.

I'm open to internships, research collaborations, and any project where I can learn fast and contribute meaningfully.

First ML Project — 97% Accuracy
Built a Logistic Regression classifier for breast cancer diagnosis using the Wisconsin dataset. Achieved 97% test accuracy with full evaluation pipeline.
2025
Robotics & Simulation Deep Dive
Developed a MATLAB/Simulink 2-DOF arm simulation with trajectory tracking, then modelled a shaper mechanism's six-link kinematic chain in Simscape Multibody for a Theory of Machines course project.
2026 ↓
ROS 2 Arm with Camera Sensor
Simulated a full 2-DOF robotic arm in Gazebo Ignition Fortress — equipped with a live camera sensor, square trajectory control, and RViz2 visualisation. Most complex project to date.
